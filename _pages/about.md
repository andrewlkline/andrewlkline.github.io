I am currently serving as a Peace Corps volunteer in Timor-Leste. My assignment is as an Engish education teacher at a public secondary school in Venilale, Baucau Municipality. I have been posting stories and insights from my experience on my Substack blog.
I graduated in 2023 from the University of Wisconsin-Madison with a degree in linguistics and Middle Eastern studies. There my research interests focused on the sociolinguistics of Arabic and other Semitic languages. I am interested in how language-use in the Arab World relates to identity and prestige and the role of diglossia in daily life. <br />

During the 2022 Fall Semester, I studied advanced Modern Standard Arabic and intermediate Jordanian Colloquial Arabic at the Sijal Institute in Amman, Jordan. Check out a day-in-my-life that I made as part of my work-study with Sijal! <br />


In addition to Semitic languages, I have worked with the Indigenous Menominee language spoken in my home state of Wisconsin as an undergraduate researcher in the Wisconsin Indigenous Language Lab under Professor Monica Macaulay. We presented "Menominee Preverb Ordering Revisted" at the 54th Algonquian Conference in October 2022 (ppt). The full paper was published in Papers of the Fifty-Fourth Algonquian Conference: Actes du cinquante-quatrième Congrès des Algonquinistes (June 2025).
