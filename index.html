About Me

I am a PhD student in the Department of Linguistics at the University of Hawai’i at Mānoa and a recently returned Peace Corps Timor-Leste volunteer.

Current Research Focus

My research interests center on the documentation of the languages of Timor-Leste, with a primary focus on the Midiki language. Midiki is spoken in the Venilale Administrative Post, where I lived and worked for two years during my Peace Corps service.

I am particularly interested in the historical contact dynamics in the region. Specifically, I am investigating the interaction between Midiki—an Austronesian language—and the non-Austronesian (Papuan) languages spoken in Venilale and the surrounding areas.
