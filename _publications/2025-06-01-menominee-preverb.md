This paper takes a new look at preverb ordering in Menominee, an area of Bloomfield’s grammar that its editor Charles Hockett says Bloomfield did not finish (1962:214-123). The grammar presents two categories of preverbs: class 1 and uncategorized. Class 1 preverbs are frequent, generally appear in a fixed order, and most lack particle counterparts. Some do not participate in the rules for metrical structure, have irregular forms when combined with person prefixes, and show irregular or no initial change. The uncategorized preverbs are left as possibly Class 1 or possibly a distinct Class 2. We examined 1,743 instances of multiple preverbs drawn from a variety of sources spanning approximately 100 years, and created a preverb array showing all cooccurrences found. It largely confirms Bloomfield’s ordering of the Class 1 preverbs, although there are also some counterexamples to his ordering in our data set. Preverbs not classified by Bloomfield occur infrequently, and generally follow those in Class 1. There are a few which do come before a Class 1 preverb, indicating that they might also be members of that class, but the examples are too sparse to draw firm conclusions from. There are very few examples of multiple uncategorized preverbs cooccurring. The few that do may suggest a possible ordering for seven of the 43 uncategorized preverbs. We also found a handful of anomalous cases where the same preverb occurs more than once on a single verb. Future research will include looking for differences in cooccurrence over time.
